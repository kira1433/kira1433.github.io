Deepfake Video Generation using Latent Flow Diffusion and Vision

Transformer

Jan 2024 - Present

Associated Professor: Dr. Abhijit Das

Domain: Computer Vision, Deep Learning, Generative AI

This collaborative project with Dr. Abhijit Das aims to enhance deepfake video generation by integrating Latent Flow Diffusion and Vision Transformer technologies. The primary goal is to create highly realistic deepfake videos while maintaining content authenticity.

Key Points:

  • Vision Transformer Adaptation: Leveraged Vision Transformer technology to improve facial feature understanding for accurate and coherent deepfake video synthesis.
  • Latent Flow Diffusion Technique: Implemented Latent Flow Diffusion methods to ensure smooth frame transitions, minimizing artifacts and preserving realism in generated videos.
  • Model Training and Optimization: Conducted extensive model training and optimization, including parameter fine-tuning and architecture optimization, to enhance video quality and fidelity.
  • Rigorous Testing and Validation: Performed comprehensive testing and validation, including quantitative analysis with deepfake detection algorithms and metrics such as FVD and FID. Tested against diverse datasets and real-world scenarios to evaluate video realism and robustness.

Technologies Used: Python, PyTorch, Vision Transformers, Diffusion Models.

Accepted in the 2nd Workshop and Challenge on DeepFake Analysis and Detection at CVPR 2024 (DFAD 2024).

DRL-based Video Streaming System

Testbed

Jul 2023 - Jan 2024

Associated Professor: Dr. Paresh Saxena

Domain: Deep Reinforcement Learning,
Video Streaming, Network Simulation

Collaborated with Dr. Paresh Saxena, Assistant Professor, to develop a testbed for a Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL)-based video streaming system, focusing on development and network simulation.

Key Points:

  • Created a basic video streaming service hosted on Railways to evaluate DRL algorithms performance.
  • Utilized Puppeteer to manually simulate various network conditions, enabling thorough testing and analysis.
  • Leveraged NS-3 to simulate 5G and Satellite networks, providing realistic environments for algorithm training.
  • Implemented algorithms such as Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) and Gradient Shared A3C to optimize video streaming performance.

Technologies used: Python, PyTorch, Docker, Dash JS, NS-3.
